On the floor
Where the numbers come from

Every calculation maps to a real measurement on a real machine. ISO-standard technical illustration — not stock photography.

01

Mount & zero

Workpiece clamped in the vice, probe zeroed on the datum face. Fixture repeatability ≤ 2 μm.

02

Probe the points

Touch-off at points A, B and C along the measured edge. Three readings per point, averaged.

03

Read the deviation

Deviation from nominal 25.000 mm is read directly — checked against ±0.050 mm (ISO 2768-m).

04

Feed the stack

The measured tolerance becomes an input to SC-008 — the same number you see in the report below.

Standards & honesty
What we reference — and what we are not

Trust comes from stating limits as clearly as capabilities.

Referenced methodology

  • ISO 286-1Tolerance grades — reference for deviations
  • ASME Y14.5Statistical tolerancing context (RSS)
  • AIAG SPCCpk definition — model-derived here, not measured
  • AWS D1.1 / EN ISO 2553Weld sizing context

How to read our results

  • Validated against closed-form worst-case / RSS invariants
  • Calculated under stated distribution assumptions
  • Applicable within 1D linear stack-ups
  • Predicted capability — not observed process capability

Known limitations — stated on purpose

  • 1D linear stacks only; no correlation model between dimensions yet.
  • Per-part sigma is derived from tolerance, not a measured-process input.
  • Results are predicted from drawing tolerances — an engineering preview, not a substitute for measured SPC or a licensed engineer's sign-off, and not valid for production approval on their own.
